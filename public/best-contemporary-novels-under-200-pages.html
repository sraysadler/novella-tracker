70 Best Contemporary Novels Under 200 Pages

Via Literary Hub · ranked by consensus

# Cover Title Author Pages Notes Description
1 Sula🛒 Toni Morrison 192 Most canonical modern literary work on this list An enduring female friendship and rivalry in Ohio's "Bottom," showcasing the full complexity of female characters.
2 Invisible Cities🛒 Italo Calvino 165 Most influential on writers and intellectual culture Postmodern classic framed as Marco Polo describing imaginary cities to Kublai Khan — gorgeous, complex, esoteric.
3 Train Dreams🛒 Denis Johnson 116 Best American novella here An American epic in miniature — a turn-of-century logger retreats into the Idaho wilderness after losing his family to wildfire.
4 The House on Mango Street🛒 Sandra Cisneros 101 Most culturally durable A forever classic about a girl growing up in Chicago's Latino neighborhoods, exploring identity and belonging.
5 The Vegetarian🛒 Han Kang
tr. Deborah Smith		 188 Biggest global literary crossover A woman's decision to stop eating meat spirals into deeper withdrawal from the world, exploring bodily autonomy and transformation.
6 Autobiography of Red🛒 Anne Carson 149 Most formally innovative Novel in verse retelling a Greek myth, erasing the rules about what novels can be through innovative form.
7 The Lover🛒 Marguerite Duras 117 Best prose style Assured, severe novel originally planned as an annotated photo album — described as a lake without a bottom.
8 Dept. of Speculation🛒 Jenny Offill 177 Most influential recent literary style Fragmentary, poetic novel about marriage and mind with luminous observations on contemporary life.
9 The Sense of an Ending🛒 Julian Barnes 163 Most elegantly constructed Man Booker Prize winner exploring memory, aging, and what constitutes a good life through melancholic reflection.
10 Convenience Store Woman🛒 Sayaka Murata
tr. Ginny Tapley Takemori		 176 Best entry point for contemporary literary fiction Dry, funny novel about a woman devoted to her convenience store job, blending love story and psychological thriller.
11 A Pale View of Hills🛒 Kazuo Ishiguro 192 Quietly one of the most psychologically sophisticated Beautiful, subtle debut narrated through the recollections of an aging Japanese woman living in England.
12 The Days of Abandonment🛒 Elena Ferrante
tr. Ann Goldstein		 188 Most emotionally devastating A woman unravels after being abandoned — her editor calls it the real Ferrante, the true masterpiece.
13 Speedboat🛒 Renata Adler 193 Biggest writer-cult reputation Discursive novel about New York and an elliptical mind — witty, singular, a bible for certain readers.
14 Leaving the Atocha Station🛒 Ben Lerner 181 Definitive millennial intellectual novel A poet in Madrid not writing poetry — one of the most subtly hilarious novels with propulsive energy.
15 When We Cease to Understand the World🛒 Benjamín Labatut
tr. Adrian Nathan West		 192 Most likely future canon climber Fictionalized accounts of 20th-century scientists exploring the darker implications of discovery — Haber, Heisenberg, Schrödinger.
16 The Loser🛒 Thomas Bernhard
tr. Jack Dawson		 190 Most admired monologue form Possibly the best ill-tempered monologue in contemporary literature, exploring failure and obsession with Glenn Gould.
17 So Long, See You Tomorrow🛒 William Maxwell 145 Pure literary craftsmanship Slim autobiographical novel by the New Yorker's longtime fiction editor — winner of the 1982 National Book Award.
18 Fever Dream🛒 Samanta Schweblin
tr. Megan McDowell		 189 Best literary horror blend Unsettling narrative about obsession and motherhood, centered around environmental poisoning and a child's fate.
19 The Mezzanine🛒 Nicholson Baker 145 Best extreme-observation novel Hilarious, cerebral debut set over a single escalator ride, containing multitudes of observations and cultural criticism.
20 Grief is the Thing with Feathers🛒 Max Porter 128 Best grief narrative Lovely, surrealist novel offering one of the most convincing and original stories about grief ever written.
21 Minor Detail🛒 Adania Shibli
tr. Elisabeth Jaquette		 105 Politically and formally severe Two-part novella covering a 1949 atrocity and a modern search for answers — a haunting, spare work.
22 Ghost Wall🛒 Sarah Moss 144 Most tension per page Teenager Silvie endures a forced Iron Age reenactment in rural England led by her domineering father, building toward horror.
23 Signs Preceding the End of the World🛒 Yuri Herrera
tr. Lisa Dillman		 128 Most linguistically compressed Fable-like narrative about crossing the Mexico-US border, exploring the borders between worlds, words, and people.
24 Lucy🛒 Jamaica Kincaid 164 Sharpest voice Semi-autobiographical coming-of-age story about a West Indian teenager working as an au pair in the US, discovering independence.
25 The Buddha in the Attic🛒 Julie Otsuka 144 Best collective narration Uses collective first-person narration to tell the story of Japanese "picture brides" arriving in California.
26 Desperate Characters🛒 Paula Fox 180 Most underrated near-classic All-time favorite about a woman who may or may not have rabies — a quietly devastating 1970 novel.
27 Open Water🛒 Caleb Azumah Nelson 160 Strongest recent lyrical debut Debut about two Black artists in love, using sensual prose and daring second-person to explore race and masculinity.
28 Small Things Like These🛒 Claire Keegan 116 Most morally precise A coal merchant discovers horror at a Church-run convent — a slim novel exploring choice and societal pressure.
29 Near to the Wild Heart🛒 Clarice Lispector
tr. Alison Entrekin		 194 Most intimidating masterpiece Lispector's debut follows Joana's life through sentences sometimes inscrutable, sometimes wild, sometimes transcendent.
30 Sweet Days of Discipline🛒 Fleur Jaeggy
tr. Tim Parks		 101 Coldest emotional atmosphere An actually perfect novel set in a boarding school where the narrator becomes obsessed with the mysterious new girl Frédérique.
31 We the Animals🛒 Justin Torres 125 Most explosive debut energy A celebrated debut — a skinny, barbaric yawp celebrating boyhood in grimy, luminous reality.
32 Old School🛒 Tobias Wolff 195 Best campus novel here An unnamed senior at an unnamed boarding school explores literary ambition — the perfect campus novel.
33 Visitation🛒 Jenny Erpenbeck
tr. Susan Bernofsky		 150 Best historical compression Elegiac novel about a house on a Berlin lake as both character and witness across sweeping historical time periods.
34 At Night All Blood is Black🛒 David Diop
tr. Anna Moschovakis		 160 Most feverish war novel 2021 International Booker Prize winner — a Senegalese WWI soldier descends into murderous madness after battlefield trauma.
35 What Belongs to You🛒 Garth Greenwell 191 Most intimate psychological realism A mesmerizing debut exploring complex desire and relationships in Sofia, Bulgaria.
36 Assembly🛒 Natasha Brown 112 Most efficient social critique A Black British finance executive with untreated cancer attends a family garden party — sparse, searing debut.
37 The Bookshop🛒 Penelope Fitzgerald 118 Most quietly perfect A perfect jewel of a novel about a woman opening a bookstore in Suffolk, ultimately evicted by the local bigwig.
38 Mrs. Caliban🛒 Rachel Ingalls 125 Weirdest emotional success A housewife falls in love with an escaped creature from a government lab — an intoxicating mix of sensuality and supernatural horror.
39 Winter in the Blood🛒 James Welch 160 Foundational Native literary work Debut about an unnamed Montana narrator seeking connection to his tribe, history, and fractured family amid disaffection.
40 Orbital🛒 Samantha Harvey 136 Most likely recent Booker-era riser Contemplative novel set on the International Space Station, following six astronauts circling the planet.
41 The Passion🛒 Jeanette Winterson 160 Most playful literary fantasy Sly historical fairy tale featuring a web-footed Venetian pickpocket and a soldier's quest.
42 Who Will Run the Frog Hospital?🛒 Lorrie Moore 160 Funniest sadness Sneakily devastating novel about a discontented woman reflecting on luminous friendship and lost teenage possibility.
43 The English Understand Wool🛒 Helen DeWitt 64 Best miniature satire Sly, delightful novella satirizing taste and manners with a distinctive heroine's voice.
44 The Witch🛒 Marie NDiaye
tr. Jordan Stump		 144 Most unsettling domestic surrealism A mother initiates her daughters into witchcraft, but the girls are unimpressed as family dynamics unravel.
45 Binti🛒 Nnedi Okorafor 95 Most influential short sci-fi work here A young Himba woman accepted to an intergalactic university must broker peace with aliens in this sci-fi novella.
46 Mariette in Ecstasy🛒 Ron Hansen 192 Most spiritually ambiguous Gorgeous, precise novel set in a 1906 Roman Catholic convent with exquisite language in service of an elusive subject.
47 Never Mind🛒 Edward St. Aubyn 197 Harshest upper-class dismantling A harrowing work of genius — the entry point into St. Aubyn's Patrick Melrose series.
48 Faces in the Crowd🛒 Valeria Luiselli
tr. Christina MacSweeney		 162 Most literary self-reflection Fresh, compelling debut about a young translator with a doubled-back perspective on artistic identity.
49 Perfection🛒 Vincenzo Latronico
tr. Sophie Hughes		 136 Best internet-age alienation Contemporary update of Perec's Things, following digital nomads in Berlin navigating internet-driven identities.
50 Point Omega🛒 Don DeLillo 117 Most polarizing prestige entry Portrait of grief refracted through art and film. A polarizing work about loss and representation.
51 The Most🛒 Jessica Anthony 144 Strong recent critical riser Cheeveresque story of a marriage told through omniscient back-and-forth narration that creates a lingering mood.
52 Why Did I Ever🛒 Mary Robison 200 Fragmentation pushed furthest Fragment novel following Money Breton, a script doctor and mother with obsessive tendencies. Funny, irreverent, and weirdly moving.
53 Elect Mr. Robinson for a Better World🛒 Donald Antrim 164 Most anarchic satire Surreal suburban nightmare about a town descending into madness and a teacher attempting restoration through suspect methods.
54 Treasure Island!!!🛒 Sara Levine 172 Most unhinged comic voice A truly insane novel about a woman who decides to live by Robert Louis Stevenson's principles of boldness and independence.
55 Sylvia🛒 Leonard Michaels 123 Most emotionally raw memoir-fiction Autobiographical novel recounting the author's marriage to the abnormally bright but depressed Sylvia Bloch, told in matter-of-fact prose.
56 Such Small Hands🛒 Andrés Barba
tr. Lisa Dillman		 94 Most claustrophobic A vicious little book about an orphaned girl — language like walls closing in.
57 Ghosts🛒 César Aira
tr. Chris Andrews		 141 Purest Aira entry A builder's family squats in an unfinished apartment building populated by visible ghosts in this playful, imaginative work.
58 On the Calculation of Volume (Book I)🛒 Solvej Balle
tr. Barbara Haveland		 176 Highest future-upside reputation First volume of a celebrated Danish septology — a woman unable to escape November 18th, a meditation on consciousness.
59 Machine🛒 Susan Steinberg 149 Most under-read experimental work Elliptical novel about a tragic summer involving a drowning — an undersung work deserving modern classic status.
60 The Orange Eats Creeps🛒 Grace Krilanovich 172 Most punk-energy book Rule-breaking, weird punk novel — a careening, side-elbowing nightmare everyone should read.
61 Killing Stella🛒 Marlen Haushofer
tr. Shaun Whiteside		 80 Sharpest knife-edge novella A brutal little novella exploring domesticity and silence — quick and sharp as a kitchen knife.
62 Mapping the Interior🛒 Stephen Graham Jones 112 Strongest genre-horror entry Coming-of-age horror story about menace, memory, and hope from a prolific genre-manipulating writer.
63 McGlue🛒 Ottessa Moshfegh 160 Rawest debut Debut novella about a transient drunk — reads like swashbuckled spray, raw and unforgettable.
64 In the Cut🛒 Susanna Moore 179 Most psychologically dangerous Dark psychological novel capturing a range of emotions and impulses rarely committed to paper.
65 Saint Sebastian's Abyss🛒 Mark Haber 160 Most Bernhard-adjacent A novel of obsession and art — a narrator travels to his rival's deathbed, told through circuitous, hilarious prose.
66 Transcription🛒 Ben Lerner 144 Most essayistic A poetic, genre-less meditation on mortality, technology, and truth.
67 Ring Shout🛒 P. Djèlí Clark 192 Most conceptually entertaining Horror reimagining where The Birth of a Nation functions as a spell summoning literal Ku Klux demons.
68 Margaret the First🛒 Danielle Dutton 160 Most niche-literary admired First-person account of 17th-century writer Margaret Cavendish — a glinting dagger of a novel.
69 Slowness🛒 Milan Kundera 176 Lesser Kundera, still substantial Metafictional meditation on modernity, memory, and performance in contemporary life.
70 Hôtel Splendid🛒 Marie Redonnet
tr. Jordan Stump		 113 Most likely to split readers hard Weird, charming novel about three sisters maintaining a hotel determined to sink — part of a loose trilogy.
