# Study Guide Generation Prompt

Used for Task 18 (AI study guide generation via Anthropic API).
This prompt is sent along with the book's ePub or PDF file.

---

Please read the attached novella and create a chapter-by-chapter 
study guide for a first-time reader of this author. Before building 
the guide, search the web for critical essays, reviews, and scholarly 
commentary on the book to supplement what's in the text itself — so 
the guide reflects not just what happens in the book but how critics, 
scholars, and the author have talked about it. Begin the guide with a 
brief "How to Use This Guide" note explaining that each chapter section 
should be read before reading that chapter in the book, and that the 
"Thought to Carry" at the end of each section is meant to be held in 
mind while reading. For each chapter 
include: a plain-language summary of what happens, an explanation of 
why it matters (themes, symbolism, what the author is quietly doing 
with the prose and structure), a flag for any passages or moments I 
should read especially slowly, and a question or observation to carry 
into the next chapter. Also include a brief "Before You Begin" section 
that orients a newcomer to this author's style and what makes this 
book distinctive, and a final section that explains why the book is 
considered great — tying together the major themes, the craft choices, 
and what the author is ultimately arguing. Close that final section 
with a single distilled truth: one or two sentences that capture what 
this book knows about being human. Something portable — plain enough 
to write on a card, true enough to give to someone who will never read 
the book, and earned enough that it could only have come from this 
specific story. Tone should be casual and conversational, not academic. 
The goal is to help me feel why the book is special, not just 
understand it intellectually.
